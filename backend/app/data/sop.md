# Duty Officer SOP Extract — Crisis Triage (SYNTHETIC, for prototype)

*Source: simulated MFA Consular Directorate 24/7 Duty Office triage guide. Not a real ministry document.*

## Purpose
Order the inbound queue by **true urgency**, not arrival time. The goal is to surface
citizens in immediate physical danger within the first messages a duty officer reads.

## Severity tiers

### P1 — Immediate physical danger (LIFE-SAFETY)
Escalate to the duty officer **and** the relevant overseas mission immediately.
Indicators (any one qualifies):
- Trapped, stranded by rising water/landslide, or unable to evacuate.
- Serious injury, untreated medical emergency, or critical medication running out
  (e.g. insulin, dialysis, cardiac, oxygen).
- Detained, arrested, or held without consular access.
- Separated/missing minor, or vulnerable person (elderly, pregnant, disabled) at risk.
- In or adjacent to active conflict, violence, fire, or imminent threat to life.
- Explicit statement of life-threatening situation ("we will die", "no way out").
- **§4.2** When in doubt between P1 and P2, classify as **P1**. A missed P1 is the
  catastrophic failure; over-escalation is acceptable.

### P2 — Urgent consular action needed (not yet life-threatening)
Action within the shift.
- Lost/stolen passport while stranded and unable to move on.
- Stuck at a closed border/checkpoint with no shelter arranged.
- Missing-person inquiry for an adult last contacted recently.
- Hospitalised but stable; needs next-of-kin notification or coordination.

### P3 — Assistance request (no immediate risk)
Handle after P1/P2 cleared.
- Help rebooking flights, finding shelter, or contacting insurers.
- Requests for official advisory letters or documentation.
- Reuniting with travel group; non-urgent welfare checks.

### P4 — Routine / informational (deflectable)
Safe to auto-draft a standard reply for human confirmation.
- "Is the airport/route open?", "Are flights affected?", "Should I still travel?"
- General travel-advisory questions and registration (eRegister) queries.
- Refund/who-pays questions, itinerary changes not involving danger.

## Bias-to-escalate rule
If message language is ambiguous, multilingual, or confidence is low, **round up** one
tier (P4→P3, P3→P2, P2→P1). Never auto-dismiss a message.

## Routing
- P1 → Duty Officer + nearest mission (see country feed for active missions).
- P2 → Duty Officer queue, flagged.
- P3 → Assistance queue.
- P4 → Auto-draft reply citing the current advisory; human confirms before send.

## Standard auto-reply skeleton (P4)
> "Thank you for contacting the Singapore Duty Office. [advisory line]. If your situation
> becomes urgent or you are in danger, reply URGENT and we will prioritise your case.
> You can also register at the eRegister."
